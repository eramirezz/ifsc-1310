Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Understanding Progressive Enhancement - A List Apart
Progressive enhancement is a strategy that focuses on a piece of core content and progressively enhances that content to make it more complex and precise to the target audience. The article “Understanding Progressive Enhancement” begins by providing a brief history of how the practice of progressive enhancement came to be. It also explains the differences between the graceful degradation perspective and the progressive enchantment perspective. Graceful degradation perspective focuses on user experience throughout different browsers. Progressive enchantment perspective focuses on performance and content. Both functions help enhance the sites and its performance but they do slightly differ. To help explain the functions of progressive enhancement the article uses an illustration of a peanut m&m.The m&m is made of three layers: the peanut, the chocolate and the shell. The article compares the peanut to the content, the chocolate to the presentation, and the shell to the client side scripting.
https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/what-is-progressive-enhancement-and-why-it-matters-e80c7aaf834a/
This article by freecodecamp “What is Progressive Enhancement, and why it matters” explains the importance of Progressive Enhancement and provides real world implications. I chose this article because I personally felt the need for a better understanding of Progressive Enhancement. I learn best with examples and this article added to the one from before. This article describes Progressive Enhancement as a methodology that is used for developing web applications by progressively adding more layers. This article highlights some core principles such as the importance of content and its accessibility to all web browsers. Progressive Enhancement must first provide a foundation with basic features. The article then moves on to examples of how Progressive Enhancement works. The first example is web fonts, although fonts can impact the web pages appearance greatly they can also cause a page to be slower if the user has a slow network with a heavy site. If such is the case, showing content is more important than waiting for fonts. The next example is initial html. It is preferred to load initial content from html rather than relying on scripts. Scripts are unreliable if a user is on a slow network. Overall progressive enhancement focuses on creating a strong foundation using basic web technologies that can be accessible in all browsers, which then allows for more complex features to be added.
